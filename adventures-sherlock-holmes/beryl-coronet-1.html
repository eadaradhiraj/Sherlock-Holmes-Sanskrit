As I stood one morning in our bow-window looking down the street, i said “Holmes, here is a madman coming along. It seems rather sad that his relatives should allow him to come out alone.”
एकदा प्रातःकाले अहं वातायने तिष्ठन् मार्गावलोकनरतः। तदा मया होम्सः उक्तः - "होम्स, पश्य, अत्र एकः उन्मत्त-पुरुषः आगच्छति। अतीव खेदजनकं यत् तस्य बान्धवैः सः एकाकी बहिः गन्तुम् अनुमतः।"
My friend rose lazily from his armchair and stood with his hands in the pockets of his dressing-gown, looking over my shoulder. It was a bright, crisp February morning, and the snow of the day before still lay deep upon the ground, shimmering brightly in the wintry sun. Down the centre of Baker Street it had been ploughed into a brown crumbly band by the traffic, but at either side and on the heaped-up edges of the footpaths it still lay as white as when it fell. The grey pavement had been cleaned and scraped, but was still dangerously slippery, so that there were fewer passengers than usual. Indeed, from the direction of the Metropolitan Station no one was coming save the single gentleman whose eccentric conduct had drawn my attention.
मम मित्रम् आसन्दात् अलसतया उत्थाय, निज-वस्त्रस्य पुटकयोः हस्तौ निधाय, मम स्कन्धस्य उपरि अवलोकयितुम् स्थितम्। तदा किञ्चन उज्ज्वलं शीतलं फेब्रवरी-प्रभातम्। विगतदिवसे पतितं हिमम् अद्यापि भूमौ घनं, शीत-सूर्यातप-प्रकाशितं च भूतम्। बेकर-मार्गस्य मध्ये यातायात-कारणात् हिमं मलिनं जातम्। किन्तु पार्श्वयोः पदमार्गे च यथापूर्वम् श्वेतम्। धूसरमार्गाः स्वच्छीकृताः। तथापि अतीव पिच्छिलाः। अतः तत्र न्यूनाः जनाः गताः। वस्तुतः 'मेटरोपोलिटन्'-स्थानकतः तम् जनं विहाय न कोऽपि आगतः। तस्य विचित्र-व्यवहारः मया लक्षितः।
“You surely must think I am mad, don't you?”
सः आगन्तुकः - "नूनं भवतोः अहम् उन्मत्तः इव प्रतीतः अस्मि, खलु?"
Holmes, looking calmly at the newcomer, said — “No, I can understand that you have fallen into very great trouble.”
होम्सः शान्त्या तम् आगन्तुकं विलोक्य अवोचत् - "नहि! अवगच्छामि यत् भवान् महति सङ्कटे पतितः अस्ति।"
The newcomer said — “God knows what terrible danger I have fallen into. I am wondering now how I am even still sane. My character is completely stainless. But today the day has come for me to be humiliated before everyone. That is just the external matter. I can no longer bear this mental agony! This is not just a danger for me alone; if this is not remedied, then the greatest man of our country will also fall into serious trouble.”
आगन्तुकः प्रत्यवोचत् - "देवः जानाति यद् अहं कीदृश्यां घोरायां विपदि पतितः। मम मतिः कथम् अधुनापि अभ्रष्टा इति चिन्तयामि। मम चरित्रं सर्वथा निष्कलङ्कम्। किन्तु अद्य सर्वेषां समक्षं मम अपमान-दिनम् आपतितम्। किन्तु एतत् तु बाह्यविषयकम्। एतां मानसिकीं पीडां सोढुं न शक्नोमि! एषा न केवलं मम एकलस्य समस्या। यदि समस्या-समाधानं न क्रियेत, तर्हि अस्माकं देशस्य सर्वश्रेष्ठः अपि जनः महाक्लेशे पतिष्यति।"
Holmes said — “Please be calm; then give your identity and tell us the incident openly.”
होम्सः आगन्तुकम् अवोचत् - "कृपया शान्तो भूत्वा स्वपरिचयं, स्पष्ट-घटनावर्णनं च ददातु भवान्।"
The newcomer said — “You are perhaps familiar with my name. I am a partner of the banking establishment named Holder & Stevenson of Threadneedle Street.”
आगन्तुकः प्रत्यवोचत् - " मम नाम परिचितम् स्यात्। अहं 'थ्रेडनीडल्'-वीथ्याः 'होल्डर् एण्ड स्टीवन्सन्' इति अधिकोष-संस्थायाः सहभागी अस्मि।
The gentleman controlled himself, took a breath, and started his story — “I know delay is not acceptable in this case. So, as soon as the Police Inspector mentioned your name, I set out to come here without delay. I came to Baker Street by underground rail and walked straight from there."
सः सज्जनः आत्मानं संयम्य, दीर्घं श्वसित्वा कथाम् आरब्ध - "जाने यदत्र विलम्बः न उचितः। अतः आरक्षक-निरीक्षकेण भवन्-नामोल्लेखनात्, तत्कालमेव अविलम्बेन अत्र आगन्तुं प्रस्थितः अहम्। अहं भूगत-लोहगेन 'बेकर्-वीथिं' प्राप्तः, ततः साक्षात् अत्र आगतः।"
“Anyway, listen — you surely know that in the banking business, investing on profitable terms is as necessary as increasing the number of depositors. A particularly profitable way of utilizing funds is lending money against very safe security. In the last few years, we have done much of this type of business, and many people from noble families have taken large loans from us, keeping expensive pictures and libraries as deposits.”
आगन्तुकः - "अस्तु, श्रूयताम् - भवद्भ्यां नूनं ज्ञायते यत् अधिकोष-व्यवसाये लाभप्रद-नियमैः धन-विनियोगः तथा आवश्यकः यथा निक्षेपकाणां सङ्ख्या-वर्धनम्। धन-विनियोगस्य एकः विशेषः लाभप्रदः उपायः वर्तते - सुरक्षित-बन्धकस्य विनिमयेन ऋण-प्रदानम्। गतान् कतिपयान् वर्षान्, अस्माभिः ईदृशः व्यवसायः बहु कृतः। तथा च अभिजाताः बहवः जनाः बहुमूल्यानि चित्राणि वा ग्रन्थागारान् वा न्यासरूपेण निधाय अस्माकं सकाशात् विपुलं धनम् ऋणत्वेन स्वीकृतवन्तः अभूवन्।"
“Yesterday morning when I was sitting in my office, a clerk brought a card and gave it to me. Seeing the name written on the card, I was startled. The name — know that it is specially known everywhere in this world and it is one of the highest and greatest names of England. I became overwhelmed at the news of his arrival in my office. And when he entered my room, I was about to tell him that, but he did not pay heed to it and went directly to business — 'Mr. Holder, at this moment I specially need fifty thousand pounds. Yes, I have heard that if good things are kept as a deposit, you lend money. So I came.'”
सः आगन्तुकः - "ह्यः प्रातः अहम् कार्यालये उपविष्टः। तदा केनचित् लिपिकेन एकं परिचय-पत्रं मह्यं दत्तम्। पत्रे लिखितं नाम दृष्ट्वा अहम् चकितः जातः। नाम इदं विश्वे सर्वत्र सुपरिचितम्। अपि च आङ्ल-देशस्य उच्चतमेषु महत्तमेषु च नामसु अन्यतमम्। मम कार्यालये तस्य आगमन-वार्तया अहम् अभिभूतः जातः। मम कक्षे तस्य प्रवेशे मम आश्चर्यं निवेदयितुम् उद्यतः जातः अहम्। किन्तु सः तद् अविगणय्य साक्षात् कार्य-विषयम् उपस्थापितवान् अभूत् 'होल्डर-महाशय, अस्मिन् क्षणे मम पञ्चाशत्-सहस्र-पौण्ड्-मुद्राणां विशेषा आवश्यकता वर्तते। आम्, मया श्रुतं यत् उत्तमानि वस्तूनि न्यासरूपेण रक्षित्वा भवन्तः धनम् ऋणरूपेण यच्छन्ति। अतः अहम् आगतः।'"
Holder said, — “For how long do you need the money?”
अलक्षेन्द्र-होल्डरः - सः व्यक्तिविशेषः मया एवं पृष्टः - "कियत्-कालं धनमिदं भवता अपेक्ष्यते?"
The gentleman said, — “I will return the money to you with interest this coming Monday. But I want the money right now.”
सः व्यक्तिविशेषः प्रत्यवोचत् - "आगामिनि सोमवासरे सव्याजं धनं प्रत्यर्पयिष्यामि। किन्तु धनं मम इदानीमेव आवश्यकम्।"
Holder said — “If that amount of money was in my personal fund, I would have been very happy to give it to you. But I do not have that means. And if I have to give it on behalf of this institution, then considering my partners, generally all the precautionary measures we take, the same will be done in your case.”
अलक्षेन्द्र-होल्डरः अवोचत् - "यदि एतत् धनं मम व्यक्तिगत-कोषे अभविष्यत्, तर्हि प्रसन्नतया भवते प्रादास्यम्। किन्तु तादृशी मम शक्तिः नास्ति। संस्थायाः पक्षात् अपि, सहभागिना सह विचार्य एव यथा-नियमं सतर्कता-व्यवस्थानन्तरं च धनं दीयते।
The gentleman said, "I want that too." Picking up his black leather bag kept beside the chair, he said, "You must have surely heard the name of the Beryl Coronet?" When the box was opened, that valuable object was seen inside on red velvet. He said, "There are thirty-nine huge beryls in this. And the value of this gold is difficult to calculate. I have asked you for money amounting to only half of the minimum value of this coronet. I want to keep this with you as collateral. Mr. Holder, from what I have heard about you, I have developed sufficient faith in you. You must keep it so carefully that no rumor spreads about it outside, and furthermore, no harm comes to it. Needless to say, this is a national treasure; even a slight scratch on it would cause a huge scandal. Losing a single beryl means losing the entire coronet, because even if you search the whole world, you won't find a match for these beryls. Of course, I trust you, and on the strength of that trust, I am leaving this with you. I will come personally on Monday morning, repay the money with interest, and redeem it."
तेन व्यक्तिविशेषेण उक्तम् यत् एवमपि तस्य आशयः। सः आसन्दस्य पार्श्वे स्थितं कृष्णवर्णं चर्मपेटिकां गृहीत्वा अवोचत्— "वैडूर्यमुकुटस्य नाम श्रुतं खलु भवता?" उद्घाटितायाम् पेटिकायाम् अन्तः रक्तवर्ण-वस्त्रस्य उपरि तत् अमूल्यं वस्तु दृष्टम्। तेन व्यक्तिविशेषेण उक्तम् — "अस्मिन् एकोनचत्वारिंशत् विशालानि रत्नानि सन्ति। एतस्य सुवर्णस्य मूल्यं गणयितुं दुष्करम्। अस्य मुकुटस्य न्यूनतमस्य मूल्यस्य अर्धमेव धनं भवतः सकाशात् याचे। बन्धकरूपेण मुकुटम् एतं भवते समर्पयितुम् इच्छामि। होल्डर-महाशय भवद्विषये श्रुतेन मयि पर्याप्तः विश्वासः सञ्जातः। तथा सावधानतया एषः रक्ष्यताम्। हानिः स्यात् चेत् जनरवः भवेत्। एषः तु राष्ट्रीयसम्पत्तिः। अस्याः स्वल्पापि हानिः महतीं विपत्तिं जनयिष्यति। एकस्य रत्नस्य नाशः नाम पूर्णस्य मुकुटस्य नाशः, यतो हि समग्रे विश्वे ईदृशानि रत्नानि न लप्स्यन्ते। अवश्यम् अहं भवति विश्वसिमि। एतस्य विश्वासस्य आधारेण एव एतम् मुकुटं भवदान्तिके स्थापयामि। सोमवासरे प्रातः अहम् स्वयम् आगत्य सवृद्धिकं धनं दत्त्वा एतं नेष्यामि।
Without thinking anything else, I called the cashier and ordered him to give him fifty thousand pounds. After the gentleman left, I began to feel somewhat afraid. If any damage occurs to this national treasure, a great turmoil will ensue.
अहम् अन्यत् किमपि अविचिन्त्य कोशाध्यक्षम् आहूय पञ्चाशत्सहस्रमुद्राः दातुम् आदिष्टवान् अभूवम्। तस्मिन् व्यक्तिविशेषे गते भीतः जातः अहम्। यदि अस्याः राष्ट्रीयसम्पत्तेः हानिः भवेत् तर्हि महान् कोलाहलः भविष्यति।
I began to feel that I had made a gross mistake by agreeing to keep this with me. Anyway, there was no time to think about such things then, so I kept it in my personal safe and focused on my work again. At the same time, I felt that keeping this valuable object in the office was not safe. Bank safes are often broken into and robbed. So, after bringing the precious object home and locking it in my bureau, I felt relieved.
एतं मुकुटं स्वीकृत्य मया महती त्रुटिः कृता इति मया अनुभूतम्। यत्किमपि भवेत्, एतत् सर्वं चिन्तयितुं तदा समयः न भूतः। अतः मुकुटं निज-मञ्जूषायां स्थापयित्वा पुनः कार्ये मग्नः अभूवम्। सहसा मया चिन्तितं यत् अयम् अमूल्यः मुकुटः कार्यालये सुरक्षितः नास्ति। वित्तकोष-मञ्जूषा-चौर्य-प्रयत्नाः बहुशः जायन्ते। अतः तत् अमूल्यं वस्तु गृहम् आनीय निजकपाटे निधाय अहं निश्चिन्तः जातः।
Now I feel it necessary to tell you a little about myself. My three maids have been working in my house for quite a few years. However, another beautiful maid named Lucy Parr has been working for me for only a few months. Perhaps because of her, a few men loiter around the house. I am a widower. My only son is Arthur. The boy has gone to the dogs. I now realize to the bone what a mistake I made by indulging him from the beginning. I wanted Arthur to join my business. The boy is a bit scatterbrained and stubborn by nature. At a young age, he became a member of a famous club and, mixing with rich people there, learned how to squander money. Gradually, he got addicted to gambling and racing. Squandering money on these bad habits, he would repeatedly come to me asking for his pocket money in advance. More than once he tried to get out of this company, but under the influence of a friend named Sir George Burnwell, he had to return to that circle every time. Sir, Burnwell has come to my house many times. The gentleman is older than Arthur and a very shrewd man. Just as his appearance is glamorous, he is also very expert in making conversation with people. I knew that this extremely cunning man should not be trusted at all. Mary also agreed with me on this matter. And yes, it is necessary to give a little introduction to Mary. Mary is my niece by relation, but since that brother of mine died five years ago, I have raised her like my own daughter. Of sweet nature, the girl bears all the burden of looking after and managing my household. Mary is my right hand. I can't think what I would do if she weren't there. In only one matter has she been disobedient to me. My son loves her with all his heart and has proposed marriage to her twice. Each time she has refused him. I think that if anyone could have drawn him into the right path it would have been she, and that his marriage might have changed his whole life; but now, alas! it is too late—forever too late!

“'You have called me names enough,'' said he, 'I will not stand it any longer. I shall not say another word about this business, since you have chosen to insult me. I will leave your house in the morning and make my own way in the world.'
अलक्षेन्द्र-होल्डरः - आर्थरेण प्रत्युक्तम् - त्वया यथेष्टम् आरोपितः अहम्। इतः परं न सहे। त्वया अपमानितः अहम् अस्मिन् विषये इतोऽपि कमपि न वक्ष्यामि। प्रातः तव गृहं त्यक्त्वा लोके स्वमार्गं रचयिष्यामि।"
“'You shall leave it in the hands of the police!' I cried half-mad with grief and rage. 'I shall have this matter probed to the bottom.'
अलक्षेन्द्र-होल्डरः - शोक-कोपाभ्याम् अर्धोन्मत्तेन मया आक्रन्दितम् - "आरक्षकाणां हस्ते विषयः त्यक्तव्यः। अस्य विषयस्य मूलतः अन्वीक्षणं कारयिष्यामि।"
“'You shall learn nothing from me,' said he with a passion such as I should not have thought was in his nature. 'If you choose to call the police, let the police find what they can.'
अलक्षेन्द्र-होल्डरः - आर्थरेण प्रत्युक्तम् अस्वाभाविकेन आवेगेन - "मत्तः किमपि न ज्ञास्यते। यदि त्वम् आरक्षकान् आह्वातुम् उद्यतः, तर्हि आरक्षकान्वेषणं भवतु नाम।"
“By this time the whole house was astir, for I had raised my voice in my anger. Mary was the first to rush into my room, and, at the sight of the coronet and of Arthur's face, she read the whole story and, with a scream, fell down senseless on the ground. I sent the housemaid for the police and put the investigation into their hands at once. When the inspector and a constable entered the house, Arthur, who had stood sullenly with his arms folded, asked me whether it was my intention to charge him with theft. I answered that it had ceased to be a private matter, but had become a public one, since the ruined coronet was national property. I was determined that the law should have its way in everything.
अलक्षेन्द्र-होल्डरः - "एतावता मम कोपाविष्टात् आक्रोशात् समग्रं गृहं जागृतम्। मेरी प्रथमा मम कक्षं वेगेन प्रविष्टा, किरीटम् आर्थर-मुखं च दृष्ट्वा सा वृत्तान्तम् अवागमत। चीत्कृत्य सा मूर्च्छिता भूमौ पतिता। मया गृहपरिचारिका आरक्षकाह्वानाय प्रेषिता। तत्क्षणमेव च अन्वेषणकार्यं तेषां हस्ते समर्पितम्। कश्चन आरक्षक-निरीक्षकः केनचित् आरक्षकेन च गृहं प्रविष्टः। तदा बद्ध-बाहुः विषण्णः स्थितः आर्थरः। आर्थरेण अहं पृष्टः यत् किम् आर्थरं चौर्येण अभियोक्तुं मम विचारः अस्ति इति। मया उत्तरं दत्तं यत् इदानीम् एषः न व्यक्तिगतः विषयः, अपि तु सार्वजनिकः जातः, यतः भग्नं किरीटं राष्ट्रस्य सम्पत्तिः अस्ति। सर्वं न्याय-प्रक्रियाधीनं भवतु इति मया निश्चितम्।"
“'At least,' said he, 'you will not have me arrested at once. It would be to your advantage as well as mine if I might leave the house for five minutes.'
अलक्षेन्द्र-होल्डरः - आर्थरेण प्रार्थितम् - "न्यूनातिन्यूनं त्वं मम तत्क्षणं निग्रहणं न कारयिष्यसि खलु? यदि मम पञ्चनिमेषान् यावत् गृहात् निर्गमनम् अनुमन्यसे तर्हि तव मम च लाभकरं स्यात्।"
“'That you may get away, or perhaps that you may conceal what you have stolen,' said I. And then, realising the dreadful position in which I was placed, I implored him to remember that not only my honour but that of one who was far greater than I was at stake; and that he threatened to raise a scandal which would convulse the nation. He might avert it all if he would but tell me what he had done with the three missing stones.
अलक्षेन्द्र-होल्डरः - मम उत्तरम् - "तव पलायनं वा चोरित-वस्तु-निगूहनं वा कथम् अनुमन्यै?" ततः स्वविषमस्थितिं परिज्ञातवता मया स आर्थरः अनुनीतः यत् न केवलं मम अपि तु मत्तः महत्तरस्य कस्यचित् सम्मानः विपत्तौ इति आर्थरेण स्मर्तव्यम्। अपि च आर्थरः स्वकृत्येन आराष्ट्र-सङ्क्षोभकरम् उपक्रोशम् उत्पादयितुं प्रभवति। लुप्तानां त्रयाणां वैडूर्याणां किं वृत्तम् इति निवेद्य आर्थरः उपक्रोशं परिहर्तुं शक्नुयात्।"
“'You may as well face the matter,' said I; 'you have been caught in the act, and no confession could make your guilt more heinous. If you but make such reparation as is in your power, by telling us where the beryls are, all shall be forgiven and forgotten.'
अलक्षेन्द्र-होल्डरः - मया कथितम् - "विषयं सम्मुखीकुर्याः चेत् वरम्। चौर्यकार्ये दृष्टस्य तव प्रतिपादनेन अपि दोषः न गरीयान् भवितुम् अर्हति। वैडूर्याणां स्थितिं निवेद्य यथाशक्ति प्रायश्चित्तं कुर्याः चेत् सर्वं क्षान्तं विस्मृतं च भवेत्।"
“‘Keep your forgiveness for those who ask for it,’ he answered, turning away from me with a sneer. I saw that he was too hardened for any words of mine to influence him. There was but one way for it. I called in the inspector and gave him into custody. A search was made at once not only of his person but of his room and of every portion of the house where he could possibly have concealed the gems; but no trace of them could be found, nor would the wretched boy open his mouth for all our persuasions and our threats. This morning he was removed to a cell, and I, after going through all the police formalities, have hurried round to you to implore you to use your skill in unravelling the matter. The police have openly confessed that they can at present make nothing of it. You may go to any expense which you think necessary. I have already offered a reward of £ 1000. My God, what shall I do! I have lost my honour, my gems, and my son in one night. Oh, what shall I do!”
अलक्षेन्द्र-होल्डरः - "तव क्षमा याचमानेभ्यः दातव्या इति सोल्लुण्ठं मत्तः विमुखो भूत्वा आर्थरः प्रत्यवोचत्। मया अवगतं यत् आर्थरः मम वचनैः अप्रभाव्यम् काठिन्यं गतः। केवलः एव एकः मार्गः अवशिष्टः जातः। मया निरीक्षकः आहूतः, आर्थरः च तस्य निग्रहाय समर्पितः। तत्क्षणमेव न केवलम् आर्थर-शरीरस्य, अपि तु आर्थर-कक्षस्य, आगृहस्य च प्रत्येकं संभावित-रत्न-निगूहन-स्थानस्य भागस्य अन्वेषणं कृतम्। किन्तु किमपि न प्राप्तम्। अपि तु अस्माभिः तर्जितः अनुनीतः अपि आर्थरः किमपि न अवोचत्। अद्य प्रातः आर्थरः काराकक्षं नीतः। अहं तु आरक्षिकाणां सर्वाः औपचारिकताः समाप्य, शीघ्रं भवत्समीपम् समस्या-शोधने भवत्-कौशल-प्रयोगाय प्रार्थयितुम् आगतः। आरक्षकैः स्पष्टीकृतं यत् साम्प्रतं किमपि कर्तुं न शक्यते। भवान् यथेच्छं धनव्ययं कर्तुं शक्नोति। मया पूर्वमेव सहस्रस्य पौण्ड्-मुद्राणां पारितोषिकं घोषितम् अस्ति। हे ईश्वर, किं करवाणि! एकस्यामेव रात्रौ मम सम्मानः, मम वैडूर्याणि, मम पुत्रः च हारिताः। हा, किं करवाणि एव!"
He put a hand on either side of his head and rocked himself to and fro, droning to himself like a child whose grief has got beyond words.
सः शिरसः उभयपार्श्वयोः हस्तौ निधाय आत्मानं पुनः पुनः दोलितवान् अभूत्। अवर्ण्य-शोकान्वितेन बालेन इव अव्यक्तं विलपितम् तेन।
